Let's Explore Rio Carnival

About Rio Carnival

The Rio Carnival is a yearly festival in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil that's considered the world's biggest Carnival celebration:

When it happens:

The festival takes place annually before Lent, usually starting on the Friday before Ash Wednesday.

What it includes

The festival features colorful parades, street parties, and Samba rhythms:

Parades: The main attraction is the parades at the Sambadrome, where samba schools perform on different nights. The most prominent parades are on Sunday and Monday.

Street parties: There are over 400 free street parties during Carnival week. Some popular parties include Bloco das Carmelitas, Corda da Bola Preta, and Simpatia é Quase Amor.

Main Attraction In Carnival:

Samba Parades

Enjoy the dazzling performances of samba schools.

Street Parties

Join the famous "Blocos" with music and dancing.

Costumes & Music

Admire the vibrant costumes and rhythmic beats.

Food & Drinks

Taste the flavors of Brazil with traditional dishes.
