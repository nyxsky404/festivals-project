Chinese New Year 2025: Year of the Snake

Interesting Facts

  • Chinese New Year is celebrated by over 1.5 billion people worldwide, including in China, Singapore, Malaysia, and parts of the West.
  • It is the largest human migration event, as people travel to their hometowns for family reunions.

    • Chinese New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is one of the most significant traditional celebrations in Chinese culture. In 2025, it falls on Saturday, January 29, marking the start of the Year of the Snake according to the Chinese zodiac.

    Rio Carnival 2025: A Spectacle of Samba and Celebration

    Fun Facts

  • The Rio Carnival attracts over 2 million people per day to its street events.
  • The event was listed in the Guinness World Records as the largest carnival in the world.
  • World Record for Samba Dancing: In 2004, Rio Carnival set a world record with 800 samba dancers

    • Dates: February 28 - March 4, 2025

    Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

    The Rio Carnival is the world's largest and most vibrant festival, known for its electrifying energy, samba parades, and colorful costumes. Held annually before Lent, the event brings millions of locals and tourists together to celebrate life with music, dance, and cultural pride.
