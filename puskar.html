Pushkar Camel Fair

A Celebration of Culture, Color, and Camels

About the Festival

The Pushkar Fair, also called the Pushkar Camel Fair or locally as Kartik Mela or Pushkar ka Mela is an annual multi-day livestock fair and cultural fête held in the town of Pushkar near Ajmer city in Ajmer district in (Rajasthan, India). The fair starts with the Hindu calendar month of Kartik and ends on the Kartik Purnima, which typically overlaps with late October and early November in the Gregorian calendar. In 1998, over 1 million visitors came to Pushkar throughout the year. The Pushkar fair alone attracts over 200,000 visitors

Traditions

Camel races and competitions:

The fair features camel races, parades, and beauty contests.

Camel trading:

The fair's main attraction is the trading of camels, horses, and cattle. Pastoralists, such as the Raikas, display their prized animals.

Cultural performances:

The fair includes folk performances and other cultural activities.

Shopping:

The fair features a vibrant market where local artisans sell handicrafts, jewelry, and traditional clothing.

Interesting Facts

World's largest camel fair

The Pushkar Camel Fair is one of the world's largest camel, horse, and cattle fairs!

Origin

The fair originated as a celebration to mark the day when 33 million gods and goddesses consecrated the lake surrounding the temple.

Bramha Temple

The fair takes place near the Bramha Temple, one of the few temples in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma.

Cultural extravaganza

The fair is a showcase of Rajasthan's culture, with folk dances, music, art exhibitions, and puppet shows.
