About La Tomatina

La Tomatina is a yearly tomato-throwing festival in Buñol, Spain, that's known as the world's biggest food fight:

When it takes place:

The festival is held on the last Wednesday of August. In 2024, it will be on August 28.

How it started:

The festival began in 1945 after a fight broke out between teenagers during a parade.

What happens:

Participants throw overripe tomatoes at each other in the streets for about an hour.

What happens before the fight:

The town hall provides breakfast of rolls and pastries. A huge pole is covered with soap and has a leg of ham on top. The most daring try to climb the pole to get the ham. Buckets of water are also thrown from balconies. A shot is fired to announce the start of the fight.

Traditions

Tomato fight

The festival's main event is a one-hour tomato fight where participants throw squashed tomatoes at each other.

Feasting

The day often ends with music, dancing, and feasting in the streets. Buñol's gastronomy includes hearty stews, seafood paellas

Cleaning

After the fight, locals wash down participants and the streets with hoses, or go to the nearby river to rinse off.

Interesting Facts

Origin

La Tomatina began in the 1940s as a street fight between friends. A boy threw vegetables at people after a street fight,

Location

The festival takes place in the town of Buñol, in the Valencia region of Spain.

Tamatos

Around 40 metric tonnes of tomatoes are used.

Tomatoes keep you clean

Tomatoes are apparently a natural disinfectant, meaning that after La Tomatina your skin will be cleansed of impurities.
