About Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year is celebrated on the new moon that falls between January 21 and February 20 in the Gregorian calendar.

What it's called:

Chinese New Year is also known as the Spring Festival in China and the Lunar New Year in Singapore.

How it's celebrated:

Spring cleaning: People clean their homes to get rid of bad luck.

Red envelopes: Children and unmarried adults receive red envelopes, or "hongbao", filled with money from elders. The money is meant to bring good luck and prosperity.

Lion and dragon dances: These dances are believed to bring good fortune.

Lantern Festival: The 15-day celebration ends with the Lantern Festival, when people release flying lanterns or carry them in parades.

Traditions

Red Envelopes

Giving red envelopes filled with money symbolizes good luck and prosperity.

Fireworks

Fireworks are believed to scare away evil spirits and bring good fortune.

Family Reunion Dinner

Families gather on New Year's Eve for a lavish meal, symbolizing togetherness.

Interesting Facts

Zodiac Animals

Each year is associated with a zodiac animal. 2024 is the Year of the Dragon!

Longest Holiday

Chinese New Year is the longest public holiday in China, lasting up to 15 days.

Red Decorations

Red decorations ward off the mythical beast "Nian" and symbolize happiness.

Lantern Festival

The 15th day of the New Year is celebrated with lantern displays and moon gazing.
