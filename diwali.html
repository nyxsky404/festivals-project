Diwali

Lighting diyas:

People light small clay oil lamps called diyas to symbolize the victory of light over darkness.

Worship:

People worship deities like Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity, and Lord Ganesha, the god of good luck.

Fireworks:

People celebrate with fireworks

Bhai Dooj:

  • On Bhai Dooj, sisters apply a red tilak on their brother's forehead, pray for his long life, and perform an aarti.
  • The tilak represents a sister's love and prayers for her brother's long and healthy life. The ceremony signifies the duty of a brother to protect his sister.
  • The festival has roots in Hindu mythology, particularly the story of Lord Yamraj visiting his sister Yamuna.
